Carbon math

These circles representatively illustrate the amount of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere that is unsafe, the amount that is added every year, and and the goal for annual carbon removal by 2050. The math for these calculations are based on the sources linked below and back-of-the-envelop formula for converting ppm of CO2 to GT of CO2 emissions. I am seeking a more precise formula and would welcome advice and corrections.

Current atmospheric carbon dioxide debt is 945 GT. Put another way, there are currently at least 945 more billion tons of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere than is safe for life on Earth. Here, we are using 350ppm as a safe concentration, which is widely accepted as the uppermost safe limit.

Sources: NASA, James Hansen.
