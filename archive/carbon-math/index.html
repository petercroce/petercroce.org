Carbon math

These circles representatively illustrate the amount of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere that is unsafe for life, the amount that is added every year, and the IPCC estimate of annual carbon removal required by 2050 to keep warming under 2°C.

The math for the circles is based on the sources below and a back-of-envelop formula for converting gigatonnes (Gt) of CO2 emissions into the atmosphere to ppm: 1 ppm ≈ 15Gt CO2 emissions (7.5Gt CO2 remains in the air; 7.5Gt CO2 absorbed by the ocean and biosphere). The formula is described by Klaus Lackner (on slide 7). I would welcome advice and corrections and additional sources.

Current atmospheric carbon dioxide debt is about 945 Gt. Put another way, there are currently at least 945 billion tons more of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere than is safe for life on Earth. This is current ppm minus safe ppm, assuming 350 ppm is safe.

Sources: James Hansen, NASA.

~ 945 Gt

Annual emissions globally were about 36 Gt in 2017 and are roughly the same today.

Source: Our World in Data.

~ 36 Gt

The goal for annual carbon dioxide removal to reach net zero annual global emissions by 2050 is 10 Gt. Annual removal on this scale is historically unprecedented. If we accomplish it, and dramatically slash emissions, we can expet to limit global warming to under 2°C by 2100, which is widely accepted to be the uppermost limit of warming our planet can tolerate without becoming dramatically more unstable.

Source: IPCC, Kriegler et al.
