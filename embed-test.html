What is this?

There is a new way to use Probable Futures maps: embed them on any website, in any story, as an small interactive map. It works with both the public maps and Probable Futures Pro.

How it works

Go to any Probable Futures map, position the map as you would like it, and click the embed icon on the right side. The icon looks like this: iframe

On the public maps, the button is hidden. Use this link to show the button.

These embeds can be used to show any Probable Futures map in any warming scenario focused on any place in the world. They can also include additional or custom data by using Probable Futures Pro.

Bracing for extreme heat

Some places have experienced quite a bit of heat in the past but would experience much more extreme heat in a 2Â°C warming scenario. In these places, people have historically adjusted their lifestyle to accommodate periods of extreme heat, such as taking a vacation during August, but would find it more difficult to live with longer periods.

Cairo is one such example. In Cairo, days above 38Â°C have occurred every year in the past. In a 1Â°C world, around 2017, such days happened about 73 days per year in a typical year.

But in a 2Â°C world, that number of days would increase to about 93 days per year in a typical year. On the current path of emissions, 2Â°C will likely be passed in the 2040s. The map below depicts a substantially different Cairo than the one above. How will life be different?

The people of Cairo must prepare for longer periods of extreme heat than they have experienced in the past. Are they prepared? People who live in and are familiar with Cairo are best positioned to answer that question.
